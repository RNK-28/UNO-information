üéÆ What is UNO?

UNO is the classic family card game that's easy to learn but challenging to master. The goal is simple: be the first player to get rid of all your cards by matching them to the card shown on top of the discard pile.

‚öôÔ∏è Game Setup

  1. Shuffle the 108-card deck and deal 7 cards to each player
  2. Place the remaining cards face down as the draw pile
  3. Turn over the top card to start the discard pile
  4. If the first card is a Wild or Wild Draw 4, return it to the deck and pick another
  5. The player to the dealer's left goes first, and play continues clockwise

PRO TIP

For faster gameplay with experienced players, deal only 5 cards each. This leads to more intense matches with quicker turnarounds.

üÉè Card Types & Actions

Number Cards (0-9)

Basic cards that must match the current color or number. No special effects.

Skip (‚è≠Ô∏è)

Reverse (üîÑ)

Changes direction of play. With 2 players, acts as a Skip.

Draw Two (+2)

Next player draws 2 cards and loses their turn. Can't be stacked in official rules.

Wild (üåà)

Change the current color to any color (including current one). Play anytime.

Wild Draw Four (+4)

Change color + next player draws 4 cards. Can only be played if you have no matching color.

üö® Official Rules & Penalties

  • UNO Call: When you have 1 card left, you must shout "UNO!" before the next player begins their turn. If caught, you draw 2 cards.
  • False UNO: If you call UNO but actually have multiple cards, no penalty (but prepare for some teasing!).
  • Challenge Draw 4: If you suspect someone illegally played Wild Draw 4, you can challenge. If guilty, they draw 4 instead. If innocent, you draw 6!
  • No Matching Card: If you can't play, you must draw until you get a playable card. If the deck runs out, reshuffle the discard pile.

üèÜ Pro Strategies

Color Domination

Focus on collecting one color to increase play options. Having 3+ cards of one color gives you control.

Action Card Timing

Save Skips/Reverses for when opponents are down to few cards. Don't waste them early.

Wild Card Economy

Hold Wild cards until absolutely needed. Wild +4 should be your last resort to avoid challenges.

Psychological Warfare

Hesitate occasionally with playable cards to mislead opponents about your hand composition.

ADVANCED MOVE

When down to two cards where one is Wild +4, play your other card first to avoid having to call UNO before playing the +4, preventing opponents from preparing defenses.

üè† Popular House Rules

While not official, these variants are commonly used:

  • Stacking: When a +2 or +4 is played, next player can play another to pass the penalty to following player (+2 on +2 = next player draws 4)
  • Seven-O: Playing a 7 lets you swap hands with any player; playing 0 makes everyone pass their hand to the next player
  • Jump-In: If you have the exact same card as the discard pile, you can play it immediately even if it's not your turn
  • Progressive UNO: Forgetting to say UNO makes you draw cards equal to the number of cards you had when caught
